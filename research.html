Machine & Medical Vision Lab (MMVL) — AI-driven healthcare vision systems
Research Focus

The Machine & Medical Vision Lab (MMVL) focuses on building reliable and interpretable AI systems for real-world healthcare applications. Our core research is centered around medical image analysis, including disease classification, lesion detection, organ/tissue segmentation, and clinical decision support. We work on vision pipelines that can handle practical challenges such as limited annotations, variations in scanners and imaging protocols, noise, motion artifacts, and class imbalance. A key goal of our lab is to develop methods that are not only accurate on benchmarks, but also generalize well across diverse hospital environments.

Alongside this, we actively explore machine learning and deep learning approaches such as CNNs, Transformers, multimodal fusion, uncertainty estimation, and out-of-distribution (OOD) detection to improve robustness and safety. Our research also extends to computer vision problems like human pose estimation and posture monitoring for healthcare and rehabilitation settings. Recently, we have been expanding into Generative AI for healthcare, including representation learning, synthetic data generation, and foundation model adaptation. Overall, MMVL aims to create reproducible, deployable, and trustworthy AI solutions by combining strong methodology with clinically meaningful impact.
